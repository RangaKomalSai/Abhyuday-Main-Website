SocioX - Case Challenge

Abhyuday, IIT Bombay, in collaboration with Hand in Hand India, presents SocioX - Case Challenge, a platform for addressing critical social challenges through innovative solutions. Participants can register individually or in teams of up to three, tackling one of ten thoughtfully curated problem statements. Deliverables include a 12-15 slide presentation outlining key insights, solutions, and strategies, submitted via Google Drive. Shortlisted teams will present at IIT Bombay, followed by a Q&A session with judges. With ₹15,000 in cash prizes and incentives worth ₹50,000, this competition fosters impactful ideas for real-world societal transformation. Join to showcase your skills and drive change! SocioX - Case Challenge

This is a dynamic case study competition designed to foster innovative solutions for pressing real-world social challenges. This competition provides an incredible platform for participants to demonstrate their analytical and problem-solving skills while addressing critical societal issues.

Key Details

  • Team Composition: Participants can register individually or form teams of up to 3 members.
  • Problem Statements: Choose 1 out of 10 impactful and thought-provoking problem statements, carefully curated to inspire innovative solutions.

Deliverables

  1. Presentation:
    • Prepare a detailed yet concise presentation (12-15 slides maximum) addressing the selected problem statement.
    • Highlight key insights, proposed solutions, and implementation strategies.
  2. Submission Requirements:
    • Upload the presentation to Google Drive.
    • Share the Drive Link in the provided submission form.
    • The file format must be named as: TeamName_LeaderName
  3. Final Round:
    • Deliver a 10-minute presentation at IIT Bombay in an offline setting.
    • Participate in a 4 to 5-minute Q&A session with a panel of esteemed judges.

Rewards

  • Cash Prizes: The top 3 teams will share a total of ₹15,000.
  • Exciting Incentives: Worth ₹50,000 for winners and participants.

Format of Presentation

  1. Title Slide
    • Team Name and Member Details
    • Case Study Title
    • Competition and Organizer Details (Abhyuday, IIT Bombay & Hand in Hand India)
  2. Introduction
    • Brief Overview of the Problem Statement
    • Context/Background of the Issue
    • Relevance to the NGO's mission and goals
  3. Problem Analysis
    • Root Cause Analysis
    • Key Stakeholders Involved
    • Data and Insights (supported by research, surveys, or case facts)
  4. Proposed Solution(s)
    • Detailed Description of the Solution
    • Innovation and Uniqueness
    • Feasibility (technical, financial, and social)
  5. Implementation Plan
    • Step-by-Step Approach
    • Timelines and Milestones
    • Resources Required (human, financial, material)
  6. Impact Assessment
    • Expected Outcomes
    • Beneficiaries and their Benefits
    • Metrics for Measuring Success
  7. Challenges and Mitigation
    • Potential Risks in Implementation
    • Contingency Plans
  8. Budget and Funding
    • Cost Breakdown
    • Potential Sources of Funding (grants, crowdfunding, etc.)
  9. Conclusion
    • Summary of Key Points
    • Call to Action for Stakeholders/NGO
    • Vision for Long-Term Impact
  10. Appendix/References
    • Supporting Data, Charts, or Graphs
    • Citations for Research and Sources

This competition provides a unique opportunity for socially conscious minds to drive meaningful change through strategic thinking and innovative solutions. Take on the challenge, showcase your skills, and be a part of impactful social transformation!

Action Plan

A nationwide competition to find India's most promising social entrepreneurs. Action plan, the flagship competition of Abhyuday aimed at raising awareness about social entrepreneurship, Action Plan motivates the youth to solve India's most pressing social issues. Action-Plan, which revolves around the 3I's mantra: Ideate - Innovate - Implement, provides the platform to the youngsters across the country to come up with feasible and optimistic solutions to tackle social issues present in the Indian society. Started in 2012, it has rapidly expanded and last year reached to around 700+ teams of 2000+ different colleges in 50+ cities across India

OASIS

The Olympiad for Awareness in Social Innovation & Sustainability (OASIS) is a flagship initiative by Abhyuday, IIT Bombay, aimed at empowering young minds to address critical global challenges and pave the way toward a sustainable future.
... This one-of-a-kind Olympiad delves into diverse themes such as water conservation, waste management, energy efficiency, air quality, biodiversity preservation, climate action, and global warming. It highlights the importance of adopting sustainable development practices, green skills, cultural sensitivity, and staying informed about current environmental affairs.
Open to students from classes 5 to 10, OASIS provides a unique platform for young participants to demonstrate their knowledge, creativity, and commitment to sustainability. With a prize pool of X amount and exciting incentives, this Olympiad promises to be an enriching and rewarding experience for all participants.
At its core, OASIS draws inspiration from the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), fostering awareness about these globally recognized benchmarks to tackle pressing issues like climate change, poverty alleviation, and environmental degradation. Participants will explore innovative solutions to real-world problems, encouraging a spirit of social responsibility and sustainable innovation.
